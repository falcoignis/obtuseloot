# World Simulation Meta Comparison

## 1) Scope / sample size
- Inputs: small-world run, large-world run set, and open-endedness ablation run.
- Goal: separate generator balance from long-run ecosystem divergence.

## 2) Method summary
- Compared concentration and convergence metrics across simulation scales.
- Added subsystem-ablation evidence (EDE off, ecosystem controls off, trait interactions off).

## 3) Key findings
- Generator appears balanced in isolation.
- World-scale progression is not fully balanced: branch convergence rises with scale.
- Ecosystem controls and trait interactions materially affect divergence behavior.
- Current classification remains mostly designer-controlled, but not purely static.

## 4) Dominant families / branches / lineages / mechanics
- Chaos remains a frequent top family at scale.
- High-throughput branches and combat-loop mechanics retain outsized influence.
- In open-endedness runs, dominant trios stay similar across worlds except stronger entropy drop when trait interactions are removed.

## 5) Rare but viable systems
- Rare systems persist in both isolated ecology and world-lab runs.
- Persistence does not guarantee strategic influence; several branches survive as low-impact tails.

## 6) Dead or suspicious systems
- No hard dead-zone subsystem detected.
- Suspicious patterns: concentration rise in no-controls world; entropy suppression in no-trait-interaction world.

## 7) Confidence / caveats
- Scale-direction conclusions: **high confidence**.
- Fine-grained causal ranking among subsystems: **moderate confidence**.
- Final balance recommendations: **provisional** until additional reruns complete.

## 8) Actionable next review steps
1. Prioritize reruns that stress ecosystem controls and trait interaction influence.
2. Avoid broad generator family rebalance at this stage.
3. Promote turnover/entropy/concentration trend metrics to default release review checklist.
