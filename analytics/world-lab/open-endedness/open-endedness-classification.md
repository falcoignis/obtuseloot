# Open-Endedness Classification

## 1) Scope / sample size
- 4 ablation worlds, shared seed pool, multi-season runs.

## 2) Method summary
- Classification combines concentration divergence, turnover divergence, and novelty persistence.

## 3) Key findings
- Classification: **Borderline: designer-controlled with weak divergence**
- Generator-only diversity remains balanced; ecosystem divergence appears only with active subsystem interactions.

## 4) Dominant families / branches / lineages / mechanics
- Full-system world maintains broader branch entropy than ablated worlds in late seasons.

## 5) Rare but viable systems
- Rare lineage persistence is non-zero in all worlds, highest in the full system run.

## 6) Dead or suspicious systems
- No complete dead-zone world, but World C exhibits suspicious lock-in signatures.

## 7) Confidence / caveats
- Confidence: moderate; conclusions are robust across ablations but not yet across many reruns.

## 8) Actionable next review steps
- Keep classification provisional until 3-seed reruns are added for full-system and no-controls worlds.
