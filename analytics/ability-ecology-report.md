# Ability Ecology Report

## 1) Scope / sample size
- Generated artifacts: 5,000.
- Coverage breadth: 6 families / 18 branches / 12 triggers / 13 mechanics.

## 2) Method summary
- Aggregated isolated generator output into family/branch/trigger/mechanic distributions.
- Calculated diversity shape indicators (family and branch Shannon diversity).
- Flagged low-frequency but non-zero systems for viability review rather than immediate buffing.

## 3) Key findings
- Family Shannon diversity: **1.791**.
- Branch Shannon diversity: **2.889**.
- No-mutation share: **19.82%**.
- Interpretation: generator ecology is broad and healthy in isolation; observed risk is downstream convergence, not template starvation.

## 4) Dominant families / branches / lineages / mechanics
- Families: consistency (868), survival (866), precision (819), brutality (818), mobility (815).
- Branches: mobility.lane-dancer (300), chaos.paradox (300), chaos.awakened-entropy (296), mobility.relay (295), brutality.mauler (294).
- Mechanics: defensiveThreshold (409), chainEscalation (401), pulse (400), burstState (396), revenantTrigger (394).
- Interpretation: top-of-distribution branches are close enough to suggest healthy generator spread before simulation pressure.

## 5) Rare but viable systems
- precision.clock (251), mobility.fusion-slipstream (254), precision.awakened-discipline (257), survival.guardian (264), consistency.anchor (265).
- These systems are below median share but far above extinction; treat as "watch-list" systems, not emergency rescue targets.

## 6) Dead or suspicious systems
- Zero-count branches: none.
- Suspicious signal: elevated no-mutation share may reduce long-run adaptation capacity if world feedback loops already converge strongly.

## 7) Confidence / caveats
- Generator-diversity conclusions → **high confidence** (good sample, broad coverage).
- Long-run ecosystem inference from this report alone → **low to moderate confidence**.
- Caveat: does not include seasonal selection feedback, collapse pressure, or branch lock-in dynamics from world-lab.

## 8) Actionable next review steps
1. Keep generator family weights stable in the next pass.
2. Use world-lab turnover/concentration metrics to decide whether mutation pressure needs incremental uplift.
3. Re-check no-mutation share after any ecosystem control tuning.
