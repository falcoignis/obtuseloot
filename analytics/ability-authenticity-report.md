# Ability Authenticity Report

## 1) Scope / sample size
- Dataset: deterministic generator replay.
- Artifacts sampled: 5,000.
- Family coverage: 6 families.
- Primary authenticity signal: encounter-reactive ability behavior share.

## 2) Method summary
- Replayed deterministic seed-based generation and classified resulting ability definitions by whether they produce contextual/encounter-reactive behavior.
- Compared reactive-share against the current acceptance gate (>=80%).
- Cross-checked dominant families/branches against ecology and world-lab outcomes to avoid over-trusting isolated generation.

## 3) Key findings
- Behavioral ability share: **94.64%** (comfortably above gate).
- Isolation-level authenticity signal is strong; this is not currently the limiting factor for ecosystem quality.
- The bottleneck is not "can we generate authentic-feeling abilities," but "do those abilities stay diverse under world pressure."

## 4) Dominant families / branches / lineages / mechanics
- Dominant families: consistency (868), survival (866), precision (819).
- Dominant branches: mobility.lane-dancer (300), chaos.paradox (300), chaos.awakened-entropy (296).
- Dominant mechanics: defensiveThreshold (409), chainEscalation (401), pulse (400).
- Interpretation: authenticity quality is broad-based, but dominance pressure is concentrated in a small high-throughput branch set.

## 5) Rare but viable systems
- precision.clock (251), mobility.fusion-slipstream (254), precision.awakened-discipline (257).
- These are not dead systems; they are low-frequency systems that still recur often enough to remain tunable.

## 6) Dead or suspicious systems
- No dead families in this isolated pass.
- Suspicious signal: repeated top branches suggest potential late-season convergence risk when progression feedback loops are enabled.

## 7) Confidence / caveats
- Major conclusion: "authenticity gate is met" → **high confidence** (large isolated sample, stable margin).
- Major conclusion: "authenticity remains healthy under world pressure" → **moderate confidence** (requires world-lab confirmation).
- Caveat: this report does not measure power skew or retention under long-run ecosystem adaptation.

## 8) Actionable next review steps
1. Keep authenticity thresholds unchanged for now (no evidence of generator-level failure).
2. Prioritize ecology/world-lab balancing on branch lock-in and lineage concentration, not family template expansion.
3. Re-validate authenticity after any balancing that alters trait interaction or ecosystem controls.
